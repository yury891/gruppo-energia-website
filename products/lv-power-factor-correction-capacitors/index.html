LV Power Factor Correction Capacitors

Gruppo Energia manufactures six capacitor series for different harmonic levels, voltage ranges and operating conditions, from standard applications to demanding industrial networks. Selecting the correct series is essential for long service life and reliable operation.

QUICK SELECTION

Select the Right Capacitor Series

Start here. Match your network condition and harmonic level to the recommended capacitor series, then compare the series cards below to confirm the best fit for your application.

NLL is the ratio between total non-linear load power and transformer rating, used here as a practical indicator of harmonic stress in the system.

Network Condition Harmonic Level (NLL) Typical THDi Typical THDu Recommended Series
Standard networks, no significant harmonic sources < 10% ≤ 5% ≤ 3% STC
Moderate harmonic presence, variable loads < 15% ≤ 8% ≤ 4% STC HD
General industrial, automatic PFC systems < 17% ≤ 12% ≤ 5% INC
Demanding industrial, frequent switching, increased harmonic stress < 20% ≤ 15% ≤ 6% INB HD
High harmonic levels, VFDs, UPS systems and non-linear loads < 25% ≤ 17% ≤ 7% INP HD
Very demanding industrial networks with severe harmonic stress < 30% ≤ 20% ≤ 8% INA HD

THDi and THDu values are indicative and based on typical industrial installations. Final capacitor selection depends on network conditions, transformer characteristics, harmonic spectrum and system configuration.

HOW TO CHOOSE

How to Choose the Right Capacitor Series

The first selection criteria are harmonic level, operating voltage and installation duty. In clean networks, standard series may be suitable. In industrial installations with variable loads, frequent switching or non-linear loads, a more robust series is required.

INSTALLATION RISK

What Happens If the Wrong Series Is Selected

Using a capacitor series that does not match the actual network conditions can shorten operating life and reduce the reliability of the entire power factor correction system.

Excessive capacitor overheating caused by harmonic current stress

Reduced capacitor lifetime compared to the rated operating life

Premature activation of capacitor protection devices

Swelling or failure of capacitor elements in demanding conditions

Unstable operation of capacitor banks and compensation steps

Higher stress on contactors, reactors and switching components

APPLICATIONS

Typical Applications
